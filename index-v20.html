02 / 15

Problem

The ultimate business bottleneck
is human biology.

AI promised us time. We just traded doing the work for prompting the AI.

Why now: voice models crossed human-grade in 2025. Agent-to-agent protocols (A2A, MCP) are arriving. A personal agent that lives in the world is finally possible.

01 · Prompt fatigue

Today’s AI lives in a box on your screen. You feed it context, tell it what to do, babysit the output. The tool waits for you.

02 · The offline penalty

On a 12-hour flight, in deep work, asleep — your world halts. Teammates blocked, clients to voicemail, pipelines stall.

03 · The gap

We don’t need more tools to do work faster. We need an extension of ourselves to keep the world moving when we step away.

01 · Representation

It faces out as you.

Orion has its own phone number and email. It takes the calls, fields the client, and gatekeeps your time — in your tone, with your relationships in mind.

02 · Execution

It reaches into your apps.

It securely accesses your devices and apps — Mac, inbox, CRM, calendar — to fulfill the request without you lifting a finger. You wake up to a recap of work already done.

03 · Memory

It learns as it works.

Every call, email, and decision teaches Orion who you are and who matters to you. Day one it sounds neutral. Month six it sounds like you.

Reachable by you to execute. Reachable by the world to represent.

06 / 15

What powers it

Four layers.
One runtime.

Not a feature inside an app. An agent with its own identity, its own memory, and its own thread of time. No incumbent has stitched these together for a person — the combination is the category.

01 · Personal entity

Its own phone number and email. A real identity people can reach — not tied to any one platform.

02 · Relationship layer

The social graph behind every action. Per-person memory of tone, history, and guardrails — built passively from every call and email. The asset that compounds; the reason a competitor’s copycat starts cold.

03 · Continuity

State that survives the session. Morning on the phone, evening on the laptop — same thread. Memory persists across conversations, surfaces, and time. Context never resets.

04 · Real-world reasoning

The judgment engine. Confidence threshold per action — drafts, pauses, escalates, or executes by stakes. Knows when to speak, when to stay quiet, when to act on its own. Never guesses with your name.
