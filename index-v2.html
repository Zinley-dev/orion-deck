🧰

Many agents per device, not one

Each device can run multiple specialized agents — a screen agent, a file agent, a voice agent — each handling its own domain. This isn't one monolithic process. It's a team of agents on every device you own.

🔒

Cross-device, not remote control

This is not "message your agent on Telegram and it clicks buttons on your laptop." Agents on your phone, desktop, and home devices act simultaneously — in parallel — coordinating through an encrypted mesh in real time.

🌐

One shared mind

Every agent — across every device — shares the same memory, context, and understanding of you. They don't just coexist. They collaborate.

What this looks like in practice:

"Prepare for my trip tomorrow." — Your desktop agent books the flight and downloads boarding passes. Your phone agent sets alarms and checks you in. Your home agent adjusts the thermostat and arms security. All at once. Not sequentially.

"I'm running late." — Your phone's voice agent calls the restaurant to push the reservation. Your desktop's calendar agent reschedules the next meeting. Your car agent re-routes. Three devices, five agents, one command, all simultaneous.

You're presenting on your laptop. — Your desktop's screen agent drives the slides. Your phone agent silences everything but flags a truly urgent call — answers it, explains you're presenting, takes a message, and surfaces it to your desktop the moment you finish.

Why this is defensible

This is why we say
"the Apple of AI agents."

Apple didn't win by making a better phone. They won by building an ecosystem where every device makes every other device more valuable. We're building the same thing for AI agents.

📈

Compounding network effect

Each new device adds multiple agents to the mesh. Every agent makes every other agent more capable. A 3-device setup isn't 3 agents — it's 9+ agents that all collaborate. The value compounds exponentially with every device added.

🔐

Security as architecture

E2E encrypted inter-agent communication is a protocol-level decision, not a feature checkbox. Competitors would have to redesign from the ground up. 71% of non-users cite trust as a barrier — we solve it structurally.

📐

Ecosystem lock-in

Once dozens of agents coordinate across your devices with shared memory and learned preferences, switching means losing all of them at once. Same reason people stay in Apple's ecosystem — it's not one product, it's the interconnection of everything.

🌏

Hardware-agnostic Apple

Apple's ecosystem requires Apple hardware. Orion's ecosystem works on any device — any laptop, any phone, 70+ hardware boards. Larger TAM. Faster adoption. No hardware dependency.
